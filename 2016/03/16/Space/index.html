Space Penetration

Given a restrictive volume of 9m×12m×18m at the the entrance of the Chinese University of Hong Kong , this multi-function space not only diverts people and dividing space, but also allows diverse penetration in and out of itself.

With the concept of creating space by carving out, a series of 4 interlocked blocks are carved out from the given volume so that a distinct organization of two types of spaces, the carved-out as penetrating and the left as flowing can satisfy the needs of spaces for different functions in multi-purpose space. Constructing these blocks with 3 kinds of materiality: Opaque concrete walls are constructed for outdoor spaces; Translucent blocks are built of glass bricks for exhibition spaces; Hollowed walls, is laid with concrete bricks in certain direction.

This design project is developed from a pure perspective of creating space, reflecting my primitive thoughts about architecture. Bearing this in mind, I am always continuing to deepen my understanding of space.

Concept: Several blocks can be interlocked to create conversion medium of light, i.e., views or perceptions of another space.
Organization: The carved-out as penetrating and the left as flowing can satisfy diverse activities in this multi-functional space.
Materiality: Concrete brick walls, hollowed in certain direction, can modify light coming in with the move of sun or changes in viewing angle.
Construction: Opaque concrete walls are constructed for outdoor spaces; Translucent blocks are built of glass bricks for exhibition spaces.
