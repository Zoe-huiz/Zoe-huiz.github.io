Philosophia kai Historia - The Rise and Fall of Athens

Different views on the Long Walls from Acropolis to Piraeus

Herodotus

As Herodotus said via the mouth of Croesus:

Now, if you think that you and the army that you lead are immortal, I have no business giving you advice; but if you know that you and those whom you rule are only men, then I must first teach you this: menâ€™s fortunes are on a wheel, which in its turning does not allow the same man to prosper forever.

The fate of man and empire is like a wheel: when it reaches the summit, it would fall then. When the Persia empire gathered thousands of soilders, when Xerxes threw the fetters into the sea, no one could foresee that Persia would fall so rapidly.

Persia is the mirror of Athens. During the invasion from Persian, the Athenes built the first two piece of Long Walls from Athens down to the harbors of Phaleron and Piraeus to protect themselves. Then they built the third Long Wall in the middle to join the two place.
